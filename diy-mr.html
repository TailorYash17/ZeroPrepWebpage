🔥 Heat the Oil 🔧 Prep

Take 3/4 amount of butter/oil from pouch 1 and add it into the cooker pot. Heat on medium flame until the oil shimmers (about 1 minute). This indicates the oil is ready for sautéing.

🔥 Heat until oil shimmers
2

🧄 Fry the Garlic 👨‍🍳 Cook

Add pouch 2 (Chopped Garlic) to the hot oil and fry, stirring, until it turns slightly golden and fragrant (about 30–45 seconds). Be careful not to burn it.

🧑‍🍳 Stir continuously to prevent burning
3

🧅 Sauté Onion 👨‍🍳 Cook

Add pouch 3 (Chopped Onion). Sauté on medium, stirring occasionally, until the onion becomes golden brown and soft (2–3 minutes).

🥘 Cook until soft and golden
4

🥫 Add Tomato Paste 👨‍🍳 Cook

Add pouch 4 (Tomato Paste) and cook for 1 minute, stirring so it blends with the onion-garlic base.

🥣 Blend with the base
5

🍚 Add Rice & Coat 🥄 Handle Gently

Add the drained basmati rice from pouch 5 into the cooker. Gently fold the rice so each grain gets coated with the tomato-onion base.

🥄 Don't crush grains
6

🥬 Add Veggies, Tomato & Water 💧 Liquid & Fresh

Add cut veggies from pouch 6 and chopped tomato from pouch 7. Fold them through the rice mixture so they sit evenly. Pour 1½ cups of measured water into the cooker. Add salt to taste and give a gentle stir to combine.

🧂 Taste and adjust salt
7

🌶️ Add Spices 🧂 Season

Add pouch 8 (Spice Box) and mix very lightly. Taste the liquid and adjust salt if required.

🌶️ Mix lightly with spices
8

🔒 Seal & Cook 🔥 Pressure Cook

Close the cooker lid properly and cook on medium heat until you hear about 3 whistles. Then reduce to low heat and cook for 5–7 minutes. Turn off the heat and let the pressure release naturally for 5–7 minutes before opening the lid. (If using a pot, cover and simmer until rice is cooked and water absorbed.)

⏱️ Let natural pressure release 🍲 For pot: simmer covered

🔔 Quick Tips:

  • 🥄 Don't over-stir after adding rice to keep grains intact for fluffy texture.
  • 🥄 Adjust water slightly depending on rice type—soaked rice needs slightly less water.
  • 🧈 For a richer taste, replace part of the water with warm stock or add a small knob of butter/ghee.
🍽️

Finish & Serve

Open the cooker once pressure has released naturally. Gently fluff the rice with a fork to separate the grains. Squeeze juice from pouch 9 (Lemon) over the rice and toss lightly. Garnish with extra coriander if available and serve hot. Pair with raita or a fresh salad.

Quick Tips ✅

🌶️ Aromatic Option

Add cumin or chile pepper while heating oil for authentic Mexican aroma.

🧈 Richer Taste

Swap part of water with warm stock or add a knob of butter/ghee for creamier richness.

🥄 Don't Over-stir

Once rice is added, fold gently to avoid breaking grains. This keeps the texture fluffy and separate.

🛢️ Water Adjustment

Adjust water based on rice type. Soaked rice absorbs less water, so use slightly less water.
